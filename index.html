Research Interests

My research interests are in developing and analysing methods for joint modeling of longitudinal and time-to-event data. I have also worked on mathematical statistics problems related to notions of exchangeability for survival processes as well as network modeling. More recently, I have begun work on just-in-time adaptive interventions with applications in mHealth.

Bio

I am a postdoctoral researcher in the Department of Statistics at the University of Michigan , starting August 2015. Before my postdoc, I received my PhD in Statistics at the University of Chicago in 2015, advised by Peter McCullagh . Prior to graduate school, I worked in the Financial Strategy Group in the Investment Banking Division at Citi.
