Research Interests

My research interests are in developing and analysing methods for joint modeling of longitudinal and time-to-event data. I have also worked on mathematical statistics problems related to notions of exchangeability for survival processes as well as network modeling. More recently, I have begun work on just-in-time adaptive interventions with applications in mHealth.

Bio

I am currently a postdoctoral researcher at the Department of Statistics at University of Michigan, starting in January 2017. I received my PhD in Electrical Engineering (Signal Processing) at the University of Michigan in January 2017, advised by Alfred Hero III and Shuheng Zhou. My B.S. (magna cum laude) and M.S. degrees in Electrical Engineering were received from Wright State University in 2011 and 2012 respectively. I have had research internships at the US Air Force Research Laboratory (2011, 2012, 2013, 2014) and at M.I.T. Lincoln Laboratory (2015).
